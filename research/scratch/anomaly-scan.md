# Anomaly Scan

## Scan Target
Dollard's Q = Psi*Phi framework and its relationship to Lagrangian electrodynamics

## Anomalies Detected

### Anomaly 1: Dimensional Correctness of a "Fringe" Claim
Dollard's Q = Psi*Phi has dimensions of Weber*Coulomb = V*s*A*s = J*s = action. This is dimensionally identical to the Lagrangian action integral. Fringe claims are usually dimensionally wrong or undefined. This one is correct.

**Assessment**: This suggests Dollard extracted a genuine mathematical relationship from classical EE (possibly from Steinmetz's work), then overinterpreted it. The dimensional correctness is not novel -- it is inherent in SI units -- but identifying it as significant is a correct observation that standard EE education typically does not emphasize.

### Anomaly 2: The h^1 = -1 Tautology
Dollard's constraint h^1 = -1 is logically equivalent to h = -1 in any algebra. Yet Dollard treats h as if it were a novel operator with non-trivial properties. The notation creates the illusion of algebraic depth where there is none.

**Assessment**: This is a critical notational trap. The versor algebra's apparent richness comes from writing h where one could write -1. The Lean proof that h = -1 is the only consistent interpretation exposed this precisely.

### Anomaly 3: The Gap Between Z_4 and Cl(1,0)
Dollard's constraints force h into Z_4 (4th roots of unity). But the properties he DESCRIBES (h^2 = 1, h acting as a kind of reflection) are more naturally satisfied by the split-complex unit j in Cl(1,0). These are genuinely different algebras:
- Z_4: {1, i, -1, -i}, cyclic group of order 4, no zero divisors
- Cl(1,0): {a + bj | j^2 = 1}, has zero divisors, is 2D over R

If Dollard had used the split-complex j instead of -1, his algebra would be genuinely non-standard. But he also wants h*j = k (where j^2 = -1), which requires mixing two different number systems. This is not impossible (Cl(1,1) does exactly this) but Dollard never goes there.

### Anomaly 4: Fortescue's 100-Year Non-Transfer
Symmetrical components have been used in EE since 1918 and have NEVER been applied to non-EE multichannel data (neuroscience, finance, vibration analysis). The DFT, which is the general form of Fortescue's transform, is universally applied. But the specific interpretation of polyphase decomposition into sequence components appears to be EE-exclusive.

**Assessment**: The N-Phase project's application to EEG is potentially the first cross-domain application. This is significant independent of Dollard.

### Anomaly 5: W = dQ/dt -- Energy or Power?
Dollard claims W = dQ/dt with W in Joules. If Q has units of J*s, then dQ/dt has units of J = energy. This is dimensionally correct. But in standard physics:
- dS/dt (time derivative of action) relates to the Hamiltonian (H = -dS/dt in Hamilton-Jacobi theory)
- The Hamiltonian is the total energy of the system
- So dQ/dt = energy is consistent with Hamilton-Jacobi theory

This means W = dQ/dt may actually be a (notational variant of) the Hamilton-Jacobi equation, not a replacement for E = mc^2. The claim of novelty is false, but the dimensional relationship is real.

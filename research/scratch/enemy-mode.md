# Enemy Mode: Steelman Dollard, Then Attack Everything

## The Steelmanned Dollard Framework

If I were the most sophisticated possible defender of Dollard, here is how I would argue:

### Steelman Argument

"Dollard's contribution is not algebraic (Z_4 is trivially standard) but ONTOLOGICAL.
He is saying that the electromagnetic energy of a circuit should be understood as the
product of its two fundamental extensive quantities -- magnetic flux and electric charge
-- rather than as the time integral of power (W = integral(P dt) = integral(VI dt)).

These two formulations are dimensionally equivalent but conceptually different:
- The power-integral view treats energy as accumulated work (thermodynamic perspective)
- The flux-charge view treats energy as the 'size' of the electromagnetic state
  (geometric/topological perspective)

Dollard's Q = Psi*Phi is the latter. It is the electromagnetic analog of the phase-space
area J = q*p in mechanics. While this is 'known' in Lagrangian mechanics, it is NOT
how practicing electrical engineers think. Steinmetz taught phasor analysis (steady-state,
frequency domain). Dollard is attempting to recover the Lagrangian perspective that was
lost when EE diverged from physics in the early 20th century.

The versor algebra, while trivially Z_4, serves a pedagogical purpose: it makes the
4-fold structure of the telegraph equation visible (RG, XB, XG, RB correspond to the
four quadrants of the ZY product). The h operator, even as -1, labels the 'real part'
of the product, making the algebraic structure explicit rather than implicit.

The extraordinary claims (aether, E=mc^2 replacement) are regrettable overreach, but
the core insight -- that EE should reconnect with Lagrangian mechanics -- is genuine
and important."

### Why This Steelman Is Dangerous

It is persuasive because it separates the defensible core (Lagrangian circuit theory is
underused in practical EE) from the indefensible claims (aether, over-unity). A
sophisticated reader might accept this steelman and conclude Dollard is merely
"ahead of his time" rather than "wrong."

The attack must show that even this steelman overstates Dollard's contribution.

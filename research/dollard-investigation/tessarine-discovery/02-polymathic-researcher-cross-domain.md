## Phase 2 Checkpoint: First Principles Analysis

### The Central Discovery

**Dollard's versor algebra is the tessarine algebra** (Cockle, 1848). This was MISSED by mapping h to -1 and collapsing everything to complex numbers. The tessarines are a 4D commutative associative algebra with basis {1, i, j, k} where:
- i^2 = -1 (circular rotation) = Dollard's j
- j^2 = +1 (hyperbolic "rotation" / reciprocation) = Dollard's h
- k = ij, k^2 = -1 (combined)

### Novice Proxy Explanations (12-Year-Old Test)

1. **Tessarines**: Like complex numbers but with TWO different "imaginary" buttons -- one rotates, one stretches/reciprocates. Four pieces total = four quadrants.

2. **Space-Counterspace**: A forward ruler (cm) and a backward ruler (1/cm). Multiplying a forward measurement by a backward one gives a pure number. The "backward" direction is generated by the hyperbolic imaginary j (Dollard's h).

3. **Action fundamental**: The TRIP is more fundamental than the SPEED. Q = flux x charge = action = Joule-second. This IS standard Hamiltonian mechanics (confirmed by Vool & Devoret 2017).

4. **Four quadrants**: Two voltage types times two current types = four powers. In tessarine terms, these are the four basis components of the product ZY.

5. **Circular+hyperbolic coupled**: The tessarine exponential e^(ai + bj) AUTOMATICALLY produces cos, sin, cosh, sinh all at once. The coupling is algebraic, not mysterious.

6. **Z-Y mirror image**: Complex conjugation (flipping imaginary sign) vs. inversion (1/z) are different operations except on the unit circle. Dollard's "mirror image" likely refers to frequency-dependent response constrained by Kramers-Kronig (causality).

### First Principles Identified

1. Tessarine algebra IS Dollard's versor algebra (isomorphism, not analogy)
